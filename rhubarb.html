Stalking rhubarb

You have come to the right place to learn about how to stalk rhubarb! Fortunately, this activity is suitable for all ages and all levels of fitness. The well-kept secret - revealed for the first time here - is that rhubarb does not run very quickly. In fact, rhubarb does not run at all! It is as if it were rooted in place. How easy is that?!

Recipes

Ginger rhubarb ice milk

What could be more refreshing than rhubarb flavoured with ginger? Try this recipe in your favourite ice cream maker.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound rhubarb
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 cups milk
  • 2 teaspoons minced ginger
  • 1/8 cup minced candied ginger

Instructions

  1. Cut rhubarb into small pieces, after making sure that you have removed and discarded all the leaves. Remember, they are poisonous!
  2. Heat all ingredients until the rhubarb is soft. Let the mixture cool.
  3. If you like your ice milk smooth, put the mixture in a food processor or blender. If you like it chunky, leave it as it is.
  4. Freeze using your ice cream maker.
  5. Enjoy!

No-ginger rhubarb ice milk

What could be more refreshing than rhubarb not flavoured with ginger? Try this recipe in your favourite ice cream maker.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound rhubarb
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 cups milk
  • No minced ginger
  • No minced candied ginger

Instructions

  1. Cut rhubarb into small pieces, after making sure that you have removed and discarded all the leaves. Remember, they are poisonous!
  2. Heat all ingredients until the rhubarb is soft. Let the mixture cool.
  3. If you like your ice milk smooth, put the mixture in a food processor or blender. If you like it chunky, leave it as it is.
  4. Freeze using your ice cream maker.
  5. Enjoy!
